How might Triller build a tool to help A&R‚Äôs find new talent?
Key Findings
I lead a multi-phase study to gather insights and suggest creative ideas for Triller to create a scouting tool for A&Rs (reps of a label that find new talent). Below you‚Äôll find a summary of the top findings as well as links to a report for the studies conducted: 1-1 Interviews, Journey Map, Competitive Analysis, Usability Study, and Codesign.
#1 ‚Äî A&R‚Äôs want a tool that empowers their gut feeling
The current landscape of tools are time-consuming, hard to understand and frankly, in an age where artists are buying fake views/fans, A&Rs don‚Äôt fully trust them. Not only that, but since everyone has the same data, the data no longer becomes a differentiator between labels who are often pitted against each other in bidding wars. A&R‚Äôs are continuously relying on their gut to make decisions and the current landscape of tools isn‚Äôt able to inform that gut decision from a qualitative perspective as that is not their focus and sentiment analysis on these tools do not factor in slang to really understand what people are saying about an artist. We have an opportunity to use our collection of intimate video-based data to empower an A&Rs gut feeling.
#2 ‚Äî A&R‚Äôs want a tool that adapts to their always-on work schedule
A&Rs expressed that the search for new artists never really turns off, spending many nights going to shows to see potential talent live. In our sessions, they expressed wanting an easy-to-use mobile-first tool that would help them scope out more artists per day. Being that the hunt for new artists is a fun activity to A&Rs (and often what drew them to the field) we have the opportunity to create an engaging tool that can be used in moments throughout their day like when they‚Äôre in line for a show or before they go to bed. With well-timed notifications we can build usage habits throughout the day.
#3 ‚Äî A&R‚Äôs see unique value in Triller to find cross-over acts
A&Rs saw potential in Triller‚Äôs ability to help them understand the ‚Äúcultural value‚Äù of a song/artist through seeing its reception on the platform. They also liked the ability to find crossover acts that may have gained a following through the comedy, fashion, or dance categories on the app. A&Rs see smaller apps like Triller as a valuable source to find unsigned talent early, in contrast to bigger platforms like Spotify or Apple Music.

While this unique angle of our app provides value, A&Rs also expressed that features such as benchmarking which are present in competitor apps are table-stake features as A&Rs need a way to justify if an artist is a worthwhile investment compared to other unsigned act.
#4 ‚Äî A&Rs want to decrease friction in connecting with artists
Getting in contact with artists during engagement process is a huge pain point for A&Rs. This came up in our codesign where they suggested solutions to better inform them of the accuracy of listed out points of contact or even giving them an elevated account on Triller that allowed them to message talent directly on the app. This second suggestion is something Triller is uniquely positioned to do since artist have verified accounts on our app and is in line with the goal of our consumer app to get more artists on to the platform.

Background
The transforming role of an A&R
The role of an A&R (reps who scout talent for a label) is transforming in an age where music consumption happens at a rapid pace and a new star is born and replaced every couple weeks. A&R‚Äôs now use a new set of digital tools to find artists with bubbling online fan bases that a label sign and profit off of before they are snatched up by another label.
Triller as a breeding ground for new talent
Social video apps like Triller, TikTok, and even Instagram have become a breeding ground for songs to blow up. This has helped both established artists popularize their songs like ‚ÄúPlug Walk‚Äù and new acts rise to fame like Lil Nas X or BoyBoy West Coast. During this pivotal moment, Triller is positioned with a valuable dataset that can be productized to help A&R‚Äôs find new talent.

What is the A&R workflow for signing new talent?
Approach
I conducted 1-1 interviews as literature on the topic is quickly becoming outdated. I referenced the Jobs To Be Done Framework as it closely linked to the type of deliverables I would produce to answer our research question. Learnings from this phase of the research helped inform me on how to best interface with A&R‚Äôs during more complex methods in later phases like the co-design.

I started with cold emails and then used referrals to source A&R‚Äôs from a diverse set of labels. I created a journey map to identify pain points and areas of opportunity contextualized to the overall process, allowing a product team to identify strategic approaches to different areas on the map.
1-1 interview session with an A&R
Challenges
I made sure to ask clarifying questions to not add my own bias in interpreting the heavy use of slang from A&R‚Äôs. Over the course of the study, I adjusted my questions to hone into different phases of the artist recruitment process to better inform the journey map. I also followed up with early interviewees to fill in the blanks based on the rapport I had built with them.

What is the current landscape of A&R tools?
Approach
When doing an assessment of the market of tools, I also took into consideration platforms that were well-positioned to enter the marke based on their unique assets (ie. Instagram with their data on music posted within the Stories feature).

In communicating my results, I broke down competitors by the data sets they were pulling from and their overall strengths and weaknesses. I did this as opposed to a feature matrix as I believe in early product development it‚Äôs important to focus on strategic positioning and finding a gap in the market rather than getting caught up in features. To reinforce this approach, I also contextualized competitors in a brand matrix.
Challenges
In light of budget to buy a subscription to all the available tools, I went through sales pitch decks these companies had posted online to understand their strategic approach and value propositions. I was also able to get access to some tools from an A&R I had built rapport with.

What is A&R‚Äôs impression of Triller as an artist discovery tool?
Approach
Before building a new A&R tool, it was important to answer this question to understand what needs Triller may already be addressing as a product. I started with an open-ended scenario to see their approach to finding new talent on the platform, and then directed them towards specific features to get their feedback.
Challenges
An unconventional navigation pattern posed a problem and kept detracting from the A&Rs ability to freely navigate. Normally I might ask a team to adjust a prototype if it‚Äôs distracting from what we‚Äôre trying to test. In this case, I reconciled by helping them navigate out of a particular tricky screen if they were stuck for too long, while making sure not to give them answers on how to perform specific tasks that were core to the task at hand. This along with some reassurance that there was no pressure and that they weren‚Äôt being judged helped move things along.
